Nikola Tesla

The Lone Genius

Here's a timeline of Nikola Tesla's Life

  • 1856: Tesla is born on July 10 in the Croatian village of Smiljan.
  • 1875: Tesla enrolls in the Karlstadt (now the University of Karlstadt) in Karlstadt, Croatia.
  • 1878: Tesla transfers to the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz, Austria, where he studies electrical engineering.
  • 1881: Tesla moves to Prague, where he works as a tutor and a laboratory assistant.
  • 1882: Tesla moves to the United States, where he begins working for the Edison Machine Works in New York City.
  • 1884: Tesla strikes out on his own, establishing his own laboratory and workshop in New York City.
  • 1888: Tesla patents the Tesla coil, a high-voltage transformer that is still used in radio technology today.
  • 1893: Tesla delivers a series of lectures on his work at the Royal Institution in London.
  • 1895: Tesla demonstrates wireless power transmission using a large Tesla coil at the Franklin Institute in Philadelphia.
  • 1899: Tesla establishes a laboratory in Colorado Springs, where he conducts experiments on electricity and radio waves.
  • 1900: Tesla returns to New York City and establishes a laboratory on Long Island, where he conducts experiments on wireless communication and the transmission of power.
  • 1931: Tesla dies on January 7 in New York City.
